# The CIRCLES Framework for Product Design

CIRCLES is the most widely used framework for answering "Design a product for X" questions in PM interviews — and for structuring real product thinking on the job.

**CIRCLES** stands for:

> **C**omprehend the situation → **I**dentify the customer → **R**eport the customer's needs → **C**ut through prioritisation → **L**ist solutions → **E**valuate trade-offs → **S**ummarise your recommendation

---

## C — Comprehend the Situation

Before jumping to solutions, ask clarifying questions:
- What is the product trying to achieve?
- What platform are we designing for (mobile, web, hardware)?
- Are there constraints — time, budget, regulation?

*A PM who jumps to solutions before clarifying is a PM who builds the wrong thing fast.*

---

## I — Identify the Customer

Segment your users. Never design for "everyone."

Common segmentation dimensions:
- **Demographics:** Age, location, income
- **Psychographics:** Goals, frustrations, values
- **Behaviour:** Power users vs. casual users, new vs. returning

Pick **one primary persona** to focus on. Name them. Make them real.

*Example: "I'll focus on Priya — a 28-year-old working professional in Tier 1 cities who shops on mobile during her commute."*

---

## R — Report the Customer's Needs

Map the customer's journey and extract their core pain points.

Use the **Jobs To Be Done** lens:
- **Functional job:** What are they trying to accomplish?
- **Emotional job:** How do they want to feel?
- **Social job:** How do they want to be perceived?

List 3–5 pain points, then **rank them by severity and frequency**.

---

## C — Cut Through Prioritisation

Not all pain points deserve equal attention. Use a simple 2×2:

| | High Frequency | Low Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| **High Pain** | ✅ Build first | Consider |
| **Low Pain** | Nice-to-have | Skip |

Pick the **one** pain point your solution will nail.

---

## L — List Solutions

Brainstorm 3–5 distinct solutions for that pain point. Think across the spectrum:
- **Incremental:** Small improvement to existing flow
- **Adjacent:** Borrow a pattern from another category
- **Radical:** Reimagine the entire experience

Don't evaluate yet. Volume first.

---

## E — Evaluate Trade-offs

For each solution, assess:
- **Impact** on the user pain
- **Effort** to build (rough proxy: Low / Medium / High)
- **Strategic fit** with business goals
- **Risks:** Technical, legal, ethical

Choose the solution with the best impact-to-effort ratio that also fits the company's strategy.

---

## S — Summarise

End with a crisp recommendation:

> "I'd prioritise building [solution] because it directly addresses [pain point] for [persona], delivers [outcome], and is feasible within [timeframe]. The key risk is [X], which we'd mitigate by [Y]."

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Skipping segmentation** — "All users" is not a user
2. **Too many solutions** — Pick one and defend it
3. **Ignoring trade-offs** — Every choice has a cost; name it
4. **No success metric** — Always define how you'll measure success
